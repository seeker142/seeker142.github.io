<h2>Chapter 1: Koran Shell</h2>

Compared to the Western Region where Monica currently lived, the Eastern Region had many buildings in more subdued colors.

Its most distinctive feature was perhaps how the forests themselves were treated as part of the landscape design. Of course, other regions had forests as well, but the Eastern Region contained vast woodlands spreading from the foothills of mountains, and the density and depth of those forests were on an entirely different level.

As a result, the buildings in such lands were often designed to harmonize with nature and the surrounding forests. Count Kerbeck's mansion was no exception.

Like the residence of a noble family living in a land frequently plagued by dragons, Count Kerbeck's mansion emphasized sturdiness.

Its decorations were restrained and its construction imposing, yet the meticulously maintained gardens were beautiful both from afar and up close. Especially from spring to early summer, when flowers were in full bloom, the gardens became a spectacular sight filled with every color imaginable.

Stepping inside the mansion, Monica found herself in an entrance hall splendid enough for a great noble.

There were numerous decorations adorned with intricate carvings and warmly colored tapestries.

The painting displayed in the most prominent location depicted the Silent Witch confronting the Black Dragon.

Monica distinctly heard Isaac mutter, "That's good work."

One of the servants who had come to receive them quietly addressed Isaac.

"Your Grace, this way, please."

"Thank you. Monica, I'll see you later."

With that, Isaac followed the servant and headed toward another room.

At the moment, Isaac was dressed in plain traveling clothes and wore glasses as the Silent Witch's disciple.

However, Count Kerbeck intended to treat him as Duke Erin traveling incognito.

(If Isabelle-sama knows Ike's true identity, then naturally Count Kerbeck should know it too... probably.)

Most likely, the reason Isaac had accompanied Monica to Kerbeck was because he had matters to discuss with Count Kerbeck.

(I'm Ike's teacher, after all...)

Monica bit her lip.

As his teacher, she needed to assure Count Kerbeck of her disciple's identity and character.

"Silent Witch-sama, this way, please. My master is waiting."

"Yes."

The person who greeted Monica in the reception room was a middle-aged man with black hair and a mustache.

He was nothing like the nobles who grew fat on indulgence and mistook luxury for dignity.

He possessed the bearing appropriate to a family that had faced dragons.

Perhaps "presence" was the best word for it.

His gaze carried both the sharpness of one familiar with combat and unmistakable intelligence.

Monica had met many powerful nobles before—

Even the king himself.

King Ambrose of the Kingdom of Ridill, who was calm and gentle yet possessed broad vision and profound wisdom.

Duke Crockford, a veteran statesman who combined the coldness and cunning of a winter lake.

Marquis Highown, Cyril's father-in-law, who possessed intelligence worthy of the Lineage of Knowledge and a keen eye for judging people.

From Count Kerbeck, Monica sensed the same intelligence and refinement those men possessed, along with something more—the unique vigor of one versed in martial matters.

"Thank you for coming such a long way, Silent Witch-sama. I am Azure Norton, Count of Kerbeck. I apologize for being unable to properly convey my gratitude in person regarding the Black Dragon subjugation."

His voice was low and heavy, filled with sincere gratitude toward Kerbeck's benefactor.

Monica straightened her back and steadied herself.

The modest reporting skills she had cultivated as the student council treasurer of Serendia Academy finally proved useful.

"No, rather, thank you very much for answering Louis-sa—Mage Count Miller's request. We were able to complete our mission safely thanks to Isabelle-sama's efforts."

"If I was able to be of some assistance to Silent Witch-sama, then I am honored."

At Monica's words, Isabelle lowered her head modestly.

She behaved as the perfect lady, seeming like an entirely different person from the one who had been excitedly showing off snow sculptures earlier.

This was a political setting.

There was no room for indulgence.

Reminding herself of that fact, Monica considered how she should bring up Isaac's circumstances.

The first to speak was Count Kerbeck.

"There could be no greater joy than being able to repay even a fraction of the debt we owe to you, Silent Witch-sama."

"Yes."

At last, they were getting to the main topic.

As Monica swallowed nervously, Count Kerbeck said...

"I must apologize for the lack of sufficient backlighting."

"..."

"In winter, the daylight hours are short, so it is difficult to find the perfect timing for proper backlighting."

Monica struggled to find a response.

Why was he apologizing to her about insufficient backlighting?

Still, it would not do to be rude.

Putting on a composed expression, Monica asked seriously:

"What... significance does backlighting have?"

"Surely it is indispensable when creating the proper atmosphere of a villain."

"..."

"I considered darkening the room and dimming the candelabras, but that would have created the atmosphere of a secret meeting rather than welcoming a guest."

"..."

"I also considered arranging for an orchestra, so that we could welcome our guest while still providing an appropriately villainous presentation. However, I feared that too grand a production might startle Silent Witch-sama."

Monica pushed her brilliant mind to its limits and thought as hard as she could.

In other words, Count Kerbeck had wanted to use backlighting to create a suitably villainous atmosphere.

However, due to the limited daylight hours, he had been unable to do so and was therefore apologizing to Monica... apparently.

After much internal struggle, Monica cautiously asked:

"Th-the necessity... of seeming villainous...?"

"Please, there is no need to hold back."

Hold back? What exactly was she supposed to be holding back about?

To the bewildered Monica, Count Kerbeck spoke with a thoughtful expression.

"I heard about it from Barrier Mage-sama. You seem to admire villains and even requested to play the role of the one being bullied."

(L-Louis-san...)

Faced with yet another revelation of the Barrier Mage Louis Miller's handiwork, Monica found herself utterly speechless.

"I also heard that the setting for your infiltration of Serendia Academy was something you came up with yourself."

She had nothing to do with that.

When Louis had informed Monica, who had been shut away in her mountain cabin, about the academy infiltration mission, he had said:

"I have already explained the setup to Count Kerbeck and his daughter. Count Kerbeck was absolutely delighted, saying, 'Why, this is just like a ballad!'"

Louis had probably thought, "The Silent Witch is incapable of acting normally. In that case, the role of a bullying victim is the most suitable."

He had been completely correct, but could he not have shown at least a little more humanitarian consideration?

Recalling Louis's malicious smile, Monica stared vacantly as Count Kerbeck continued with quiet enthusiasm.

"So, in order to please Silent Witch-sama, we conducted extensive research and devised a variety of refinements."

"R-really..."

"But it is a surprisingly profound field."

Monica vaguely recalled Isabelle saying something similar before.

Apparently, villainy was quite profound.

Isabelle, who shared her father's aspirations, spoke up.

"Father. I think we should go with the classic approach."

"Indeed, you are right. In that case, Silent Witch-sama..."

Monica let out a startled "Hih!" and straightened her posture.

The villainous father and daughter pair put on suitably evil smiles.

"Welcome to my domain! We are delighted to receive you! Kukuku, fuhahahahahahaha!"

"Please make yourself comfortable during your stay! Ohohohohoho!"

Monica was at a complete loss.

Overwhelmed by the villainous father and daughter, she forgot what she had intended to say.

(What was I... right, Ike... Ike...)

With a somewhat numb mind, Monica desperately tried to remember what she needed to say.

"Thank you very much for all your consideration. Um... regarding I—Duke Erin..."

"I have already heard everything from Isabelle."

Putting away his villainous smile, Count Kerbeck nodded with the bearing of a thoughtful great noble.

"I have informed the servants only that Duke Erin is visiting incognito. All arrangements have been entrusted to Agatha."

Agatha, who stood waiting by the door, bowed her head.

As Isabelle's maid, Agatha had supported Monica's infiltration mission alongside Isabelle.

If she was handling things, there should be nothing to worry about.

"...Thank you very much."

"We understand the value of your silence. We have no intention of rudely disturbing the peace that silence brings."

Count Kerbeck knew the true identity of Prince Felix Arc Ridill, the Second Prince.

And yet he was saying that he had no intention of disrupting the current situation, in which Felix had effectively retired as the Second Prince and Duke Crockford had stepped back from the forefront.

Filled with gratitude, Monica bowed deeply to Count Kerbeck.

After finishing her greetings with Count Kerbeck, Monica headed to the tea room together with Isabelle.

Waiting for them there were Isabelle's mother, Sylvia, and her younger brother, Henry.

Monica had met both of them during Serendia Academy's school festival.

"Thank you very much for everything back then."

As Monica expressed her gratitude while recalling the hectic school festival, Henry—who had grown taller since then—smiled brightly.

"It was an honor to be of help."

Henry had black hair and seemed to resemble his father, but his charming smile was very much like Isabelle's.

Monica sincerely wished he would aspire to become a beloved young noble rather than a villainous one.

Lady Sylvia Norton was an elegant noblewoman around forty years old, with orange curls much like Isabelle's, neatly arranged.

As she offered Monica some sweets, Sylvia asked,

"Have you already seen the snow sculptures?"

"Y-yes! They were really amazing...!"

"We are holding it as a competition, and our family has even prepared prizes. By all means, Silent Witch-sama, please participate in the judging."

"Eh? N-no, that would be far too great an honor..."

The two cheerfully talked about what had happened during Isabelle's absence and the current state of Kerbeck.

Isabelle also shared recent events at Serendia Academy and spoke about the efforts of the Third Prince, Albert, who had become student council president.

Though Albert was often overshadowed by the First and Second Princes, it seemed that he and his attendant Patrick were working hard every day to improve the academy.

Isabelle herself was not a member of the student council, but she occasionally offered Albert advice or fought alongside him as nothing more than an upperclassman.

While enjoying the peaceful tea time, Monica finally asked about something that had caught her attention.

"Come to think of it... those egg ornaments displayed on the side table... are they a traditional craft from this region?"

In fact, she had occasionally noticed them while walking through the streets of Kerbeck.

They appeared to be eggshells dyed with pigments, and upon closer inspection, intricate patterns had been painted on them.

Monica liked looking at orderly, repeating patterns, so whenever she saw something like this, her eyes would naturally follow it.

Isabelle answered her question.

"That is called a Koran Shell. Long ago, it is said that people began making homes from eggshells for little fire spirits who had nowhere to go during the winter."

Once upon a time, there was a tiny fire spirit whose power was so weak that it struggled to survive the winter cold.

Unable to bear seeing this, a human made a home from an eggshell and placed it in the corner of a room.

The fire spirit sheltered within the eggshell until spring arrived, and thanks to the spirit's light, the human was able to conserve fuel and make it through the winter as well.

At least, so the story went.

"In other words, it is a charm for safely making it through the winter."

Gently cupping the eggshell in both hands, Isabelle set it on the tea table.

The shell was primarily green, with several colors layered atop one another to create a design depicting a forest and deer.

"Is this painted with a brush?"

"No. Koran Shells are made by drawing patterns with wax and then immersing them in dye."

For example, if one wished to preserve part of the eggshell's original white color, that section would first be covered with wax.

The entire shell would then be dyed red. After it dried, the wax would be melted away, revealing a white pattern against a red background.

That was the basic idea.

In reality, the process was repeated many times, layering colors and creating increasingly intricate patterns.

It was handiwork that required tremendous amounts of time and effort.

Perhaps Isabelle's explanation made him want to contribute something as well, because her younger brother Henry chimed in.

"Apparently, they were originally most common in the Northern Region. They get even more snow there than in the East, so they became popular as winter handicrafts. Gradually, the practice spread to the Eastern Region as well."

The Northern Region of the Kingdom of Ridill was mountainous and cold, receiving even more snowfall than the East during winter.

Among Monica's acquaintances, the Barrier Mage Louis Miller was from the Northern Region.

Whenever snow fell in the royal capital, he always looked thoroughly displeased.

Apparently, the task he hated most in the entire world was shoveling snow.

Perhaps that was precisely why indoor handicrafts became established there.

In fact, the Eastern Region suffered more dragon-related damage than the Northern Region, so people continued agricultural work even during winter.

Because dragons hibernated during the winter, it was actually safer to work outdoors then.

(Come to think of it, I wonder if Nero is still awake. It seems colder than usual this year... maybe he's hibernating.)

As she gazed at the brightly colored Koran Shells, an idea suddenly occurred to Monica.

Could she buy some as souvenirs for Nero and Lana?

However, she did not recall seeing them displayed as merchandise in any souvenir shops.

The Koran Shells she had noticed seemed to be decorations rather than products for sale.

"Are Koran Shells not sold as souvenirs?"

At Monica's question, Sylvia set her teacup back onto its saucer and nodded.

"Since they are eggshells, they break easily and are difficult to transport. It might be different if one used the Anderson Company's vibration-resistant carriages, but those come with special fees."

At Sylvia's words, Henry spoke with admiration.

"The Anderson Company's carriages are amazing, aren't they? Refrigerated carriages, vibration-suppression carriages—they don't call them the King of Transport for nothing."

...As expected of a transportation giant.

Even Monica, who was rather ignorant of current affairs, had heard the name of the Anderson Company.

It was a major trading company based in the Southern Region of the Kingdom of Ridill that had achieved tremendous success in the field of distribution, developing refrigerated carriages for transporting food and comfortable carriages designed to minimize vibration.

It would not be an exaggeration to say that anyone involved in commerce could not afford to ignore the Anderson Company.

Sylvia narrowed her eyes as though reflecting on something.

"What truly made Anderson-san the King of Transport was not merely carriage development, but their financial support for highway construction. Anderson-san understands that improving the roads is more efficient than endlessly improving the performance of the carriages themselves."

Road construction was indispensable for the development of a territory, but it required enormous amounts of money.

Recognizing this, Anderson had offered financial assistance to local lords throughout the kingdom for the purpose of road development.

Those who benefited from such support could hardly afford to take a hard line against the Anderson Company.

Not even the lords of those territories themselves.

Suddenly curious, Monica asked:

"Um, has Anderson-san invested in Kerbeck as well?"

"If we are speaking specifically of road development, then no. Kerbeck is fully capable of funding that on its own."

There was a hidden meaning in Sylvia's answer.

She was probably wary of the Anderson Company.

For that reason, she had prevented the company from gaining influence in the area of road construction.

However, it would be impossible to exclude the Anderson Company from every business venture within Kerbeck.

There was no reason to do so in the first place, and making an enemy of a company with such influence over distribution would only create problems.

"Viewed from another angle, investment can become a form of invasion. You should remember that."

At Lady Sylvia Norton's words, Isabelle and Henry nodded deeply.

Monica found herself nodding along as well.

Just being in this room made her feel as though she were becoming smarter.

That thought drifted through a corner of her mind.

A different atmosphere hung over the reception room than when the Silent Witch had first been welcomed.

The air was quiet, yet taut with tension.

Seated across from Count Kerbeck was Duke Erin, Felix Arc Ridill.

In other words, Isaac Walker.

After being guided to a separate room from the entrance hall, Isaac had changed into clothing befitting the Duke of Erin.

There were no servants present.

After having tea brought in, Count Kerbeck had dismissed everyone.

(...Count Kerbeck should know everything that happened behind the scenes of the Supreme Council.)

Count Kerbeck's daughter, Isabelle Norton, had read the diary left behind by the real Felix.

Which meant she had likely learned both that the real Felix Arc Ridill was dead and about Duke Crockford's scheme.

She should also understand the negotiations between Monica and the King—that for the sake of national interests, Duke Crockford had merely been removed from power, while the Second Prince had been allowed to retire under the pretense that he was still the genuine article.

To put it maliciously, because of Isaac, the King had effectively handed Count Kerbeck leverage over the royal family.

Isaac's existence was an enormous spark that could threaten the very continuity of the royal house.

If he wished, Count Kerbeck could use it to extort the royal family in any number of ways.

(All of the King's subjects swear loyalty to the King... but that's nothing more than a fantasy.)

Every noble had their own position to protect, and if the survival of their territory was at stake, some would even oppose the royal family.

That was especially true of great provincial nobles.

Unlike court nobles, they were more distant from the King and had to think about the people of their own lands.

The first to speak was Count Kerbeck.

"A short while ago, I told Silent Witch-sama the following: '*We have no intention of rudely disturbing the peace that silence brings.*' That is our position."

If the truth of the Second Prince and Duke Crockford's scheme were both kept silent, the peace of the kingdom would be preserved.

Conversely, exposing everything would lead to a decline in the Kingdom of Ridill's national strength.

As Count Kerbeck, that outcome was not something he desired.

(Count Kerbeck belongs neither to the First Prince Faction nor the Second Prince Faction. He is a neutral. However, there is no doubt that he does not want war. If war broke out with the Empire, the Eastern Region would be the first battlefield.)

Exposing the truth would be disadvantageous for Count Kerbeck as well.

That was why he chose silence.

And having that stated explicitly was something Isaac appreciated.

In noble society, trust and credibility were important, but benefits were equally important.

To compare and confirm those interests, Isaac had traveled all the way to Kerbeck, and Count Kerbeck had succinctly expressed his intentions with those earlier words.

*We have no intention of rudely disturbing the peace that silence brings.*

The phrase "that silence" likely referred both to remaining silent about the truth concerning the Second Prince and to the actions taken by the Silent Witch.

By invoking the word "peace," he was also expressing opposition to war.

And the phrase "disturbing" suggested that Count Kerbeck intended to remain a third party and had no intention of acting as though he were directly involved.

Having understood the political intent behind those words, Isaac thought:

(...It is frustrating not to be able to express my gratitude honestly.)

If Isaac humbled himself too much before Count Kerbeck here, it would undermine the King's position.

And if Isaac appeared to become too close to Count Kerbeck, people might begin speculating that the great nobles of the West and East were conspiring together to threaten the throne.

That was also why this visit had been made in secret.

So instead, they confirmed each other's positions through formalities and secured mutual cooperation.

That was the game of noble politics.

Carefully seeking the fine line before cooperation became collusion, Isaac spoke.

"I would like you to take this as my personal sentiment."

"Oh?"

"I am a great fan of the Silent Witch."

Count Kerbeck stroked his mustache.

Isaac continued with a serious expression.

"Therefore, I hope to maintain a good relationship with those who supported her from behind the scenes."

A slight smile softened Count Kerbeck's face.

Isaac smiled in return.

"I have heard from my daughter. Your feelings for Silent Witch-sama are genuine."

How thorough, Isaac thought with an inward chuckle.

Of course, he had passionately sung Monica's praises in front of Isabelle fully aware that word would reach her father.

Isaac Walker was genuinely grateful to Count Kerbeck.

Truly.

Not merely because it was politically convenient.

For helping Monica. For working tirelessly on behalf of the Supreme Council. For helping maintain the deception.

No amount of gratitude would ever be enough.

But he could not openly say such things.

The least he could do was wish for a good relationship between them.

"Yes."

Nodding, Isaac grasped the hand extended toward him.

Count Kerbeck had offered his hand.

"May we continue to enjoy good relations."

Count Kerbeck's grip was powerful, once again reminding Isaac of the strength of a family that had stood against dragons.

Now that their respective positions had been made clear, what remained was an exchange of information under the guise of casual conversation.

As nobles of the West and East, there was no shortage of matters they wished to discuss.

Taking a sip of tea, Count Kerbeck spoke as though something had just occurred to him.

"Ah yes, I heard that Silent Witch-sama recently captured another escaped convict."

"The Wind Hand Mage, Adolf Faron. I hear he caused quite a commotion at Cresburn Magic University. Fortunately, your daughter was not caught up in it."

"Was Isabelle not disappointed that she could not witness Silent Witch-sama's exploits firsthand?"

"I can understand how she feels."

*So do I. I wanted to see it too... I really wanted to see it...*

Isaac kept those thoughts to himself and continued.

"I have heard that, following the incident at Neigal Prison, House Albright is working to strengthen their cursed tools."

He played one card of information—the strengthening of cursed tools.

Count Kerbeck nodded in response.

"House Albright will only become more indispensable. The previous Abyss Shaman-sama was truly masterful in monopolizing the art of cursecraft and leveraging it to its fullest advantage."

One of the Seven Sages, the third Abyss Shaman, Ray Albright, was the finest practitioner of cursecraft currently alive. However, when it came to using that expertise for the prosperity of the family, the second Abyss Shaman—Ray's grandmother—had been exceptionally talented.

The present status of House Albright existed largely because of the second Abyss Shaman's abilities.

(That said, the second Abyss Shaman is quite elderly. The third is an outstanding master of curses, but he isn't the type who's particularly skilled at governance or business. In that case, they'll likely want someone to manage the family.)

Isaac had heard a certain rumor concerning that matter, but it had not yet been confirmed.

So he deliberately avoided the subject and moved on.

"Which means there is only one escaped convict left."

"The last one, Ryud Classen, was the man who created Vikerun."

Vikerun was originally a magical medicine used as a painkiller.

However, because it caused powerful hallucinations, was highly addictive, and carried a significant risk of mana intoxication, it was heavily regulated within the Kingdom of Ridill.

Isaac had somewhat bitter memories involving Vikerun.

He had once tried to use Bishop Laviana—who was later purged in connection with the matter—to orchestrate Duke Crockford's assassination.

In the end, that only tightened the noose around his own neck.

It had been a trap set by Duke Crockford, who had realized Isaac intended to oppose him.

Since then, Isaac had engraved a lesson into his heart:

If a piece appears too convenient for you, be suspicious of it.

(...Putting that aside, Ryud Classen.)

Isaac searched his memory.

"I heard that Ryud Classen is originally from the Kingdom of Randoll. As a result, there was some friction with Randoll concerning his extradition."

At Isaac's words, Count Kerbeck nodded grimly.

"Indeed. Therefore, we have strengthened border security in anticipation of Ryud Classen attempting to flee to his homeland. However... I recently heard something that concerns me."

Count Kerbeck paused and studied Isaac.

"Does the name Samuel Throth mean anything to you?"

"I am not familiar with it. Judging by the name, is he a citizen of Randoll?"

Since he genuinely had no idea, Isaac answered honestly.

Count Kerbeck began explaining.

"He is a former knight of the Kingdom of Randoll and also comes from a prestigious family of enchantment mages. He has quite a few unsavory rumors attached to his name and is apparently an old acquaintance of the escaped convict Ryud Classen."

"And this Samuel Throth is in the Kingdom of Ridill?"

"That is what I have heard. Throth is a man obsessed with money, and it appears he intends to use Vikerun to conduct business in our kingdom."

A man seeking to make a fortune through Vikerun and the escaped convict who had developed it.

A thoroughly unpleasant combination.

Isaac had already reached a tentative conclusion the moment he heard the story, but he deliberately paused as if considering the matter before speaking.

"...Recently, even in the Western Region, salons dealing in illegal drugs have been increasing in number. I will push for stronger enforcement measures."

Drugs entering from the East would pass through the South and Central Regions before eventually reaching the West.

There was also the possibility that they could travel through western ports and reach the maritime nation of Alpatra.

(...It might be wise to keep a close eye on Sazandol as well.)

Sazandol possessed a major port, and its volume of trade far exceeded Erin's.

If anyone intended to smuggle illegal drugs, there was every possibility they would use Sazandol as a base of operations.

Count Kerbeck smiled at Isaac's response.

It was a deep smile, almost villainous in nature.

"Most reassuring. I can certainly understand why so many people rely upon Duke Erin."

(...Hmm?)

"I hear you have recently become quite close with nobles of the Northeastern Region as well."

Isaac answered Count Kerbeck's meaningful smile with the flawless smile of a prince.

"I merely offer advice. Questions such as how best to govern their territories successfully. It is a consultation far beyond someone of my youth."

"Your Grace also seems quite knowledgeable regarding the situation within the Empire."

The implication was clear.

Count Kerbeck was suggesting that Isaac had been gathering information on the Empire through the Northeastern Region.

It was hardly an uncomfortable topic to be probed about.

Anyone with even a modest amount of political sense would be doing the same.

"I am willing to share any information that proves necessary. Surely there are ways we can cooperate. For example, concerning the Anderson Company, whose influence has been growing considerably of late."

"Oh?"

Count Kerbeck's eyes sharpened.

Isaac had prepared exactly this sort of conversational gift for occasions like these.

Time to begin negotiations.

<p style="text-align: center;">＊　＊　＊</p>

After leaving the reception room, Isaac was guided to another chamber.

It was a lounge near the room where he would be staying.

Apparently Monica was there.

Under the pretext of ensuring that "the incognito Duke Erin could relax in comfort," Count Kerbeck had restricted servant access to several rooms within the mansion.

In other words, he had arranged matters so that no servants except Agatha—who knew the circumstances—would approach.

It was remarkably considerate.

Though Isaac still could not determine whether this was done out of gratitude toward Monica or to give Isaac enough freedom to reveal his own hand.

There was no doubt that Count Kerbeck genuinely felt indebted to the Silent Witch.

But Isaac did not yet know the man well enough to judge everything on that basis alone.

(As expected of one of the great nobles of the East... I've been forced to reveal quite a few cards.)

A faint weariness lingered after the negotiations.

Yet he could not allow it to show.

The smile on his face remained elegant.

That was the daily life of Felix Arc Ridill.

In the lounge Agatha led him to, Monica was examining the various items decorating the room.

After confirming that the door had closed behind him, Isaac called out to her.

"Monica."

"Ah, Ike, are you finished talking with Count Kerbeck?"

"Yeah, it was only a brief greeting."

He smiled while concealing the fact that it had actually been a rather weighty exchange of political maneuvering. At least allow him this much posturing.

"Did you enjoy the tea party?"

"Yes. Isabelle-sama's mother... Sylvia-sama is amazing. She knows so many things."

Countess Kerbeck, Sylvia Norton...

The Norton family was renowned for being well-informed and highly influential in noble society. It must have been quite educational for Monica.

Monica closed her eyes as though reviewing everything she had just learned.

"Things about politics, and distribution... I feel like I got a little smarter."

Isaac let out a small laugh.

Whenever he talked with Monica, it felt as though the tension wound tightly around him gradually loosened.

He did not dislike that feeling.

He cherished that peace, which was precisely why he worked so hard behind the scenes to protect it.

"Your intellect is a national treasure, My Master."

"There are still so many things I don't know. Oh, right! Ike, Ike, look at this. It's amazing. It's made from an eggshell and..."

Monica spun around, her skirt fluttering, and pointed at an ornament displayed atop a chest.

It was an eggshell dyed with pigments and decorated with intricate patterns.

Isaac unconsciously let out a soft breath.

"A Koran Shell. How nostalgic."

Monica looked up at him with her mouth slightly open.

Isaac tapped his pocket and called out to Wildianu.

The white lizard crawled smoothly up onto the back of his hand, and Isaac lifted it so it could see the Koran Shell.

"Beautiful, isn't it? The patterns are drawn with wax on the eggshell, which is then dyed. There's a legend that small fire spirits used them as shelters through the winter."

Wildianu stared intently at the eggshell with pale blue eyes and swished its tail.

"If I were to use a shelter, I would prefer a tub filled with water."

It required even less care than an ornamental fish.

"How modest," Isaac thought with an amused smile.

At that moment, Monica, who had been silently pondering something, spoke up.

"Tomorrow, Isabelle-sama is going to take me to meet the artisan who makes these. Ike, you should come too."

"With pleasure, My Master."

<p style="text-align: center;">＊　＊　＊</p>

Back in his study, Count Kerbeck was reviewing documents when his wife Sylvia entered.

She wished to discuss the evening banquet.

After they had gone over the guests' preferences, seating arrangements, and other details, Sylvia asked her husband:

"So, what did you think of Duke Erin?"

It was a straightforward question.

Count Kerbeck folded his fingers together in his lap and recalled his conversation with the young man.

"For someone so young, he possesses remarkable courage. Even standing before a villainous noble such as myself, he did not falter in the slightest. Duke Crockford must have trained him extensively."

That young man had been raised to do the same things Duke Crockford could do.

That fact was both frightening and pitiable.

"His mind works quickly. He knows how to draw out reactions through conversation. Above all, he understands how to move people."

"In that case, his life must be rather difficult. He was raised far too thoroughly as royalty."

Sylvia's words struck the mark.

Had the real Prince Felix lived, that young man would undoubtedly have become an excellent aide.

But reality had not turned out that way.

As long as the young man did not forget what he owed the Silent Witch, there was no need to stir up unnecessary trouble.

That was Count Kerbeck's conclusion.

"If he ever wishes to convert to the path of the Villain Prince, then we shall personally instruct him."

"My, that does sound delightful."

Naturally, if that day came, they would have to thoroughly teach him the Kerbeck style.
