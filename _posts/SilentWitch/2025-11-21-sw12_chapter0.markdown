<h2>Prologue: Welcome to Kerbeck! ~A Silent Grand Welcome~</h2>

*Machine translated from the first 60 pages of the Bookwalker preview.*

---

"...The time has come."

Count Azure Norton of Kerbeck declared in a low, weighty voice, standing with the light at his back.

"Now then, let us give our honored guest a magnificent reception! Kukuku, fuhahahahaha!"

His laughter was filled with all the imposing menace of an evil count preparing to face a hero.

The story began with a single letter. This winter, word arrived from his eldest daughter Isabelle, who attended Serendia Academy, that she would be bringing the Silent Witch, Monica Everett, home with her during her visit. Thus began the Norton family council. The agenda: how best to entertain the Silent Witch.

"Father, leave the role of host to this Henry!"

His son immediately spoke up.

Fourteen-year-old Henry, his face still retaining traces of childhood, did his utmost to project the fearless confidence of an evil young noble.

"First, we shall hold a grand banquet and make known the long-cherished feelings of the people of Kerbeck!"

The people of Kerbeck had long been desperate to give a grand reception to their benefactor, the Silent Witch Monica Everett.

"The palanquin is already prepared. Give the order at any time, Father."

Naturally, it was not for them to ride in. It was a palanquin intended for the Silent Witch.

At that moment, Countess Sylvia Norton of Kerbeck, who had remained silent until now, finally spoke.

"Wait. According to Isabelle's letter, it seems Silent Witch-sama does not wish for an extravagant welcome."

The noblewoman with orange curls much like her daughter's narrowed her eyes behind her fan.

"You are still too naive, Henry."

"But Mother, Silent Witch-sama is a modest person. Might she not simply be holding back out of consideration for us?"

"Even so, if we make a public spectacle of it, we will only trouble Silent Witch-sama."

The Silent Witch was the great hero who had defeated the two great evil dragons of the Kingdom of Ridill. Ever since unveiling the Black Chalice before the Supreme Council, she had been inundated with academic engagements and similar obligations.

And with the winter solstice approaching, she would surely prefer to spend her time quietly. Sylvia's reasoning left both father and son nodding in agreement.

Closing her fan, Sylvia smiled with a menacing elegance worthy of a villainous madam.

"So long as our desire to welcome Silent Witch-sama is conveyed, there is no need to publicize it on a grand scale."

After that preface, Sylvia presented her proposal.

Count Norton stroked his mustache and let out a rumbling laugh.

"Kukuku, excellent. Spare no expense. My people, give form to your feelings! The time has come to fulfill the long-cherished wish of Kerbeck! Fuhahahahaha!"

And thus, the people of Kerbeck's plan to welcome the Silent Witch had been progressing quietly for far longer than Monica realized—quietly, yet with undeniable passion.

"Th-this is…"

The first thing Monica saw after stepping down from the carriage and setting foot in Kerbeck was a long row of snow sculptures lining the town.

She had already been told during the journey that a new event called the Kerbeck Snow Festival was being held this winter, where the townspeople created snow sculptures however they pleased.

Having grown up in the Central Region, where snowfall was scarce, Monica was unfamiliar with such things. At most, she had occasionally seen a small snowman someone had made on a street corner.

Since it was a snow festival, she had expected to find impressive snowmen. Instead, nearly all of the works lined up before her were modeled after the Silent Witch standing against the Black Dragon.

The Silent Witch raising her staff with a dignified expression. The Silent Witch confronting a dragon. The Silent Witch praying for the peace of Kerbeck...

"Please do not worry, Onee-sama."

What exactly was she supposed to not worry about? As Monica stood frozen in bewilderment, Isabelle spoke confidently.

"The snow sculpture contest does permit written captions, but phrases such as 'Silent Witch-sama, welcome to Kerbeck!' that would hint at Onee-sama's stay in Kerbeck have been prohibited."

"*The hero who descended upon that land—at her divine visage, the people weep tears of gratitude.*"

"*The Silent Witch's judgment shattered the heavens, split the earth, and pierced the wicked.*"

"*With the Silent Witch's smile, Kerbeck's sun was restored, flowers bloomed in abundance, birds sang, and the people joined together in songs of joy.*"

Instead, the accompanying captions consisted entirely of praise for the Silent Witch's achievements. At this point, it had become so detached from the reality of the Black Dragon subjugation that it was impossible to make sense of it anymore.

While Monica stared with wide eyes, Isaac muttered quietly beside her.

"Erin doesn't get much snow, so we couldn't copy something like this…"

"…Ike?"

Publicly, Isaac was the Second Prince. He was disguised in plain clothes, wearing glasses and a scarf that concealed the lower half of his face.

Behind the glasses, his blue eyes gazed up at the snow sculptures with a touch of envy. From beneath the scarf came a quiet murmur of "Lucky…" Monica very much wanted to pretend she had not heard it.

Silent Witches to the right and Silent Witches to the left. Nearly every one of the snow sculptures depicted a Silent Witch who was taller, slimmer, and more dignified than the real Monica.

Monica, who still tended to be mistaken for someone younger than her actual age—in other words, a rather pint-sized figure—looked up with vacant eyes at the statues of the Silent Witch that had been considerably beautified compared to the original.

Isabelle's maid, Agatha, spoke modestly.

"I have heard that there was also a proposal to hold a grand banquet in order to welcome Silent Witch-sama. However, Milady was concerned that Silent Witch-sama might prefer a quiet stay... and so, in order to convey our welcome discreetly, this approach was chosen."

It certainly was quiet. But the passion poured into the snow sculptures was anything but ordinary. Even to an amateur's eye, the craftsmanship was impressive.

Isabelle looked at Monica and smiled with the elegance befitting a count's daughter who cared deeply for her people.

"These snow sculptures are the embodiment of the feelings that we, the people of Kerbeck, hold for Onee-sama."

How could Monica possibly dismiss such heartfelt enthusiasm?

"Th-thank you very, very much."

The great hero who had saved Kerbeck offered her thanks with a strained smile.

At that moment, Monica noticed a group of children watching from a distance. They were probably trying to hide, but perhaps because they were so excited, they were unable to keep their voices down completely, and their conversation reached her ears.

"It's Isabelle-sama...!"

"Isabelle-sama came back!"

"Then is the person beside her Silent Witch-sama?"

Monica stiffened and hurriedly pulled up her scarf to cover her mouth.

At the moment, her robe and staff were packed away with the luggage, and she was dressed in travel clothes chosen for warmth rather than appearance. In other words, she did not look particularly dignified.

Half of her reason for hiding her face was that she did not want to attract attention. The other half was that she feared the children would be disappointed if they learned that the Silent Witch was actually a short, unimposing little thing like her.

"Shh! Don't be so loud. It's a secret that Silent Witch-sama is coming."

"Pretend we didn't notice, pretend we didn't notice."

"I wonder if Silent Witch-sama saw the snow sculptures we made?"

Listening to their exchange, Monica felt a strange tickling sensation in her chest. It was not an unpleasant feeling at all.

As she struggled to make sense of the unfamiliar emotion, Isabelle stepped closer and spoke quietly.

"Those children are from the orphanage where I often go to read stories to them."

Isabelle was surely beloved by them. Anyone could tell just by watching the children.

They clearly wanted to speak to Isabelle, yet they were restraining themselves because Isabelle was in the middle of hosting a guest—the Silent Witch.

"Agatha, please deliver some sweets for them later."

"Yes, Milady."

Listening to Isabelle and Agatha talk, Monica found herself wanting to do something as well.

She looked up at Isaac, who stood beside her.

"Ike, could you step away for a moment?"

"...? As you wish, My Master."

After confirming that Isaac had moved a short distance away, Monica lightly raised her right hand.

She extended her index finger and swept it smoothly from left to right.

Tiny particles of ice appeared around Monica, glittering as they reflected the light of her magic power.

The ice particles rapidly grew into slender tubes.

Each ice tube was shorter than an arm.

They appeared all around Monica in a ring, swaying gently from side to side.

As the tubes collided with one another, clear tones rang out.

Riin. Riin.

One of the children murmured,

"It's the Alteria Chime...!"

Monica turned toward the children and placed her index finger against her lips.

The children let out delighted cries.

It was embarrassing, but if they were happy, then she was happy too.

(I'm glad I came to Kerbeck...)

With a warm feeling in her heart, Monica turned forward—

Only to see Isaac clutching his chest as though overcome with emotion, and Isabelle pressing at the corners of her eyes.

"The chime you performed during the New Year's magical offering were wonderful as well, but to think I would be fortunate enough to see them from this close... What a blessed disciple I am."

Even in disguise, Isaac's strikingly handsome features could not be concealed. The sight of him trembling with emotion was like a painting come to life.

"Ever since I heard about the New Year's ceremony, I have wished to hear that sound in person... To finally be able to hear it directly is truly moving. Ah... what a wonderfully pure and refreshing tone."

Tears sparkled at the corners of Isabelle's eyes.

They were beautiful, like droplets shed by a benevolent goddess.

The pint-sized witch let her gaze wander right and left before finally lowering it to her feet.

Looking at the two of them somehow made her eyes feel dazzled.

"I-I'm glad... if you enjoyed it..."

The Snow Festival, which originated in Kerbeck as an event where snow sculptures were displayed and judged in a contest, would later become established as one of Kerbeck's representative traditional festivals.

Naturally, even in the Kerbeck Snow Festivals of later years, the most popular motif remained the Silent Witch.

Every winter, snow sculptures of the Silent Witch would line the streets, and tales of her achievements would continue to be passed down.
