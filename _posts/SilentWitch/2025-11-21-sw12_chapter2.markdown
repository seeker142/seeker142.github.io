<h2>Chapter 2: Village of the Silent Witch</h2>

The day after arriving in Count Kerbecky, Monica visited the home of a Koran Shell craftsman with her apprentice Isaac, guided by Isabelle.

When Monica heard "craftsman's house," she had imagined something like a workshop, but in reality it was the living room of an elderly woman's mansion. Several tables were arranged there, and the elderly women were enjoying making Koran Shells together with their grandchildren.

"Goodness, welcome, Silent Witch-sama."

"My, how small and adorable. To think such a young lady drove away a black dragon."

"Please, make yourselves at home. We have tea and sweets ready."

The elderly women surrounded Monica and Isabelle, offering sweets as though doting on their grandchildren. Isaac, once again dressed in plain clothes and wearing glasses, stood at the back with the maid Agatha.

"Oh my, who might this handsome young man be?"

"I am their attendant."

He deflected the elderly women's questioning with ease. He was so composed that no one could possibly suspect he was the second prince of the country.

A boy who seemed to be someone's grandson looked up at Isaac and muttered, "He's tall…"

Isaac gave him a wink.

"Thanks. I'm sure you'll grow soon too. Are you here today to make Koran Shells with your grandmother?"

"I'm not really interested in that stuff. I just came for the sweets."

"Then, here you go."

Isaac flicked his hand lightly, closed it tightly, and when he opened it again, a piece of candy wrapped in paper was sitting in his palm. Just moments ago, he hadn't been holding anything at all.

"Whoa!"

The boy shouted, and the other children crowded around asking to see it too. He had completely become popular. Monica had recently noticed that he seemed to be rather fond of children. Even now, he was examining the Koran Shells the children brought one by one, saying, "That's a beautiful dye job," and "You drew that very well," as he praised them.

"Now, now, Silent Witch-sama, please come this way."

"Y-yes, excuse me…"

Monica joined the circle of elderly women together with Isabelle and sat down in front of the table.

On the table were eggshells from which the contents had already been removed. Monica picked one up and furrowed her brows in confusion.

"What kind of magic is used to remove the contents…?"

"You make a small hole here and remove the inside."

Isabelle, seated beside her, explained to Monica as she stared at the eggshell.

Indeed, the bottom of the egg was sealed with beeswax. It seemed to be to prevent dye from entering during the soaking process.

However, the size of the hole was baffling to Monica. It was smaller than the nail of her little finger.

"At that size, wouldn't the yolk get stuck…?"

Perhaps they used wind magic to manipulate the egg and draw the contents out through the hole. Or maybe water magic to flush water in and wash it out from inside.

While Monica was thinking through magical formulas for extracting the contents, Isaac, who had returned from the circle of children, spoke up.

"You just insert a bamboo skewer through the hole, stir it up, and scoop it out."

Monica looked up at Isaac, her mouth slightly open.

(Ike really has made Koran Shells before, hasn't he?)

Ever since she had seen Koran Shells at Count Kerbeck's mansion and he had said, "This brings back memories," Monica had been wondering about it.

However, asking in a place with so many people didn't feel appropriate, so she swallowed the question.

An elderly woman passed Monica a lump of beeswax and a set of tools for working with it. It resembled a thin smoking pipe. Wax was placed in the small cup at the tip, heated over a candle flame to melt it, and then used for drawing patterns.

Isabelle demonstrated, saying, "You use it like this." She seemed to have made Koran Shells several times before, and smoothly drew a deer pattern.

Monica tried it herself, melting the wax as she imitated what she had seen. Koran Shells typically used repeating patterns of simplified geometric designs.

Monica was not good at drawing, but she was skilled at copying shapes. She struggled at first with the unfamiliar tool, but as she got used to it, it gradually became enjoyable.

She reproduced the design she had memorized by sight, adjusting it to the size of the egg in her hands. She covered the areas she wanted to remain white with beeswax, then soaked it in dye. The trick to beautiful coloring was to start with light dyes and gradually deepen the color.

Once the eggshells had been placed in the dye, it was time to take a break with tea.

The tea prepared for them had a strong richness and aroma, pairing well with milk.

(It might be similar to the tea Casey prepared at a tea party long ago.)

Snow was fluttering outside the window. Warming her fingertips on the hot cup, Monica glanced sideways at Isabelle and Isaac. The two were chatting happily about something.

"Walker-sama, you seem to know so much about Silent Witch-sama. I thought I was her number one fan, but I still have much to learn."

It seemed Isabelle had even chosen introductory magic theory classes specifically to learn more about Monica.

"The more I study, the more I realize just how amazing Onee-sama is…" Isabelle murmured, and Isaac nodded as if to say, "of course, of course."

"I'm sorry, Isabelle, but I can't really yield when it comes to being the person who knows the most about the Silent Witch. That would be me, her first disciple."

As Monica gave a wry smile at the self-proclaimed "first disciple" who had so brazenly imposed himself on her, Isabelle let out a wistful sigh.

"I have been gathering information daily regarding Onee-sama, but I'm still lacking, I suppose."

"I-Iinformation gathering…?"

What on earth does she want to know about me? Confused, Monica watched as Isabelle presented the results of her information gathering.

"Apparently, when Onee-sama presented the  at an academic conference, the audience flocked in great numbers."

"Yes, it was quite a mess…"

"Recently, I hear you have been conducting joint research with the Thorn Witch-sama and have been traveling to Marquis Highown's territory."

"Yes, I recently stayed at the marquis's residence…"

At Monica's response, Isaac interjected, "Wait a moment. I recall that during joint research you usually stayed in a town near the fields, but this time you stayed at Marquis Highown's residence?"

"Ah, yes. There were various circumstances…"

It was difficult to say she had been eaten by peas. Monica wanted to present herself as a cool, capable master. A master who gets eaten by peas would be far from cool.

Trying to gloss over how she ended up staying at Marquis Highown's residence, Monica desperately changed the subject.

"Um, it was a very lovely mansion. I'd love to visit again!"

After all, that mansion was filled with books. As a result, whenever she had free time she would become engrossed in reading, to the point that Cyril scolded her for neglecting meals and sleep.

In addition, Marquis Highown, out of kindness, lent her the books she had been unable to finish during her stay. Monica had been embarrassed that they were such rare books, but Marquis Highown had said, "If someone who wants to read them this much can read them, the books will be happy." What a wonderful person.

As Monica recalled that exchange and smiled softly, Isaac unexpectedly turned expressionless.

"…Oh."

Was it just her imagination, or had that murmur sounded unusually cold?

(Maybe Ike also wanted to borrow books…?)

While Monica was thinking this, one of the elderly ladies said cheerfully, "By the way, I heard that Silent Witch-sama is fond of the hot springs in Sechen. Will you be stopping there during this visit as well?"

"…"

Letting out a blank sound, Monica tilted her head.

Sechen. A place name she had never heard before. Naturally, she had no memory of staying there.

Isaac muttered with a bitter expression, "More information I don't know about…"

But this was also information Monica did not know.

"Um, where is Sechen?"

Agatha, Isabelle's maid, quickly answered Monica's question.

"Sechen is a small village in the Ragotto territory, northwest from here, adjacent to Kerbeck. That area is known for its hot springs, and I believe Sechen also has several inns for spa visitors."

Hearing Agatha's explanation only deepened Monica's confusion.

Since leaving the mountain lodge, Monica had visited various places besides the royal capital, but she had no memory of ever staying in a village called Sechen.

When Monica honestly said so, Isabelle and Agatha's expressions changed.

"Young lady, could this be the so-called situation…?"

"Yes, indeed, this is that situation…"

What situation?

Beside the tense Monica, Isaac said in a low, sharp voice, "Is there an impostor of the Silent Witch in that village?"

"Yes, this must be… an imposter of Onee-sama."

(My… impostor?)

While Monica was completely unable to follow the conversation, Isaac, Isabelle, and Agatha began to emit an unsettling aura. All three of them had frightening eyes.

"This can't be ignored."

"Yes… impersonating Onee-sama is unforgivable."

"Milady, shall we make an appeal to the lord of that village?"

At Agatha's suggestion, Isabelle shook her head.

"First, we must accurately determine the facts… Agatha, change tomorrow's schedule."

"Understood."

"…Um, Isabelle-sama? Agatha-san?"

When Monica timidly called out, Isabelle replied meekly with an apologetic expression.

"Onee-sama, I deeply apologize. Something urgent has come up. I will ask Henry to guide you for tomorrow's sightseeing."

Sensing that stopping Isabelle here would lead to something terrible, Monica panicked. Then Isaac, looking out the window, muttered quietly,

"…Maybe I should bring a hunting rifle."

"Um, Ike, Ike…?"

Flustered, Monica forced a strained smile at Isaac.

"I'll go out tomorrow for a bit. Don't worry, it'll be settled in one night."

Isaac's smile was as gentle and warm as spring sunlight. And yet, the blue eyes behind his glasses glimmered with a chill colder than ice. She had a very bad feeling about this.

As the three of them silently burned with fighting spirit, Monica rose from her chair and raised her voice.

"I-I want to go to Sechen village!"

Thus, the next day's plans for the group were decided under the pretext of a hot spring excursion, which in reality was an investigation.

Snow lay thinly across the forest spreading along the mountain foothills, and white steam rose in thick plumes from various points. Several hot springs lay within the forest.

Hot springs were a treasure to the local residents. Though it was a remote area off the main roads, the presence of hot springs meant that spa visitors still came every year and spent money there.

However, over the past year, the number of visitors to these hot springs had sharply declined. The cause was the dense mana filling these springs—a sudden, unexplained mana contamination.

mana is something humans cannot withstand for long in places where its concentration is too high. If one were to immerse themselves in a hot spring contaminated with mana, it could even lead to death from mana poisoning.

The reason for the sudden increase in mana concentration had not been determined, and the local residents were all at their wits' end.

The forest hot spring here was also one of those contaminated by mana. Near such a hot spring, a boy was crouched down.

He looked to be around eleven or twelve years old, with a sharp, intelligent expression. He had short black hair and wore plain clothing typical of a local village boy.

The boy lifted up a sieve that had been submerged in the hot spring and quickly retrieved the items placed on it.

Once the sieve was empty, he placed the contents of a bag he had brought onto it, and again submerged the sieve in the hot spring.

Once he finished his work, he immediately moved on. The boy had a relatively high level of mana and was not easily susceptible to mana poisoning, but even so, staying in this place for too long was harmful to the body.

Eventually, once he reached the entrance of the forest where the mana concentration was lower, he spread out the items he had soaked in the hot spring water onto the snow-free ground.

The things he had soaked were truly varied: pebbles and pieces of wood picked up from around the area, forks and fragments of pottery, scraps of cloth, and more.

The boy took a cloth bag from his satchel and opened its mouth.

The contents of the bag were similar—more pebbles and such. However, these were items that had been removed from the hot spring a full day earlier.

The boy took out a tool resembling a ruler from his bag. The tip was made of metal, and when an object touched it, its mana level could be measured. It was apparently a simple mana measuring device.

The boy measured and compared the mana levels of stones just taken from the hot spring and stones that had been left for a day.

"Hmm, just as the teacher said. Even when soaked in magically contaminated hot spring water, the mana level drops significantly after a day..."

The boy took a bundle of notes from his bag and reviewed the records from the past few days.

What he was doing was soaking various substances in magically contaminated hot spring water in order to apply enchantment magic...
